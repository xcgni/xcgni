# Excogni principles (internal canon)

The values below are the product's core, stated once so every future copy, feature and design
decision derives from the same place instead of re-deriving it. User-facing wording lives on the
About page ("What we stand for"); this file is the source it radiates from.

## Three aims

1. **Staying sharp.** Practice that adapts and genuinely trains. Not busywork, not engagement
   farming: sessions exist to exercise real abilities.
2. **Insight, in the broad sense.** Numbers are the start, not the point. The product should help
   a person understand: when they do well, what helps them, what the numbers can and cannot say.
   Every insight is correlational, evidence-gated, and worded honestly.
3. **Self-knowledge.** The long promise: over months, a truthful picture of your own mind that you
   could not get any other way. This is why measurement honesty is non-negotiable: a flattering
   mirror is a useless one.

## One way of working: respect

The user is the person being measured, not the product being sold. Concretely:
- **Transparency:** methods public (methodology page), formulas inspectable, changes versioned.
- **Honest numbers:** uncertainty shown (SEM bands), provisional flagged as provisional, "we do
  not know yet" said out loud (validation status on the About page).
- **No dark patterns:** no streak guilt, no nagging (notifications opt-in, capped, factual), no
  artificial scarcity, no engagement tricks. Rest is part of a real routine.
- **Data dignity:** export anytime, delete for real (verified cascade), population statistics only
  from anonymous, consented data.

## The test every surface must pass

Before shipping any copy, feature or notification, ask: does this respect the person, and does it
serve sharpness, insight or self-knowledge? If it only serves engagement, it does not ship.
